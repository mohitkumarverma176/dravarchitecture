Landscape Design Trends in 2025

April 2025 • by Drav Architecture Team

As urbanisation accelerates across India's tier-1 and tier-2 cities, thoughtful landscape design has become a critical differentiator for residential townships, institutional campuses, and public spaces alike.

At Urban Axis — the landscape consultancy arm of Drav Architecture — we have worked across projects in Lucknow, Greater Noida, Dehradun, and Bihar, observing first-hand how client priorities and design challenges are evolving.

The best landscape is one that feels inevitable — as though it always belonged there. Achieving that requires deep listening, rigorous site analysis, and a willingness to let the land guide the design.

Native Planting & Ecological Sensitivity

One of the strongest trends we are seeing is a move towards native and regionally appropriate planting schemes. Rather than importing ornamental species that require intensive maintenance and irrigation, forward-thinking projects are now specifying plants that are naturally adapted to local climate conditions. This reduces long-term operational costs while supporting local biodiversity.

Integrating Green Infrastructure

Landscape design is increasingly expected to serve functional ecological roles — managing stormwater through bioswales and rain gardens, providing urban cooling through strategic tree canopy placement, and creating wildlife corridors within dense developments. Projects like the DPS School campus in Lucknow have incorporated these principles to create environments that are both beautiful and performant.

Green infrastructure is not just about aesthetics. When done right, it reduces heat island effects, manages runoff, and creates healthier environments for the people who live and work in these spaces.

Outdoor Living & Community Spaces

Post-pandemic, there has been a significant resurgence in demand for high-quality outdoor spaces — both within residential projects and on institutional campuses. Families want usable gardens, not just ornamental ones. Developers building in Greater Noida and Lucknow are increasingly commissioning detailed landscape masterplans that include play areas, seating nodes, walking trails, and event lawns as integral components of the built environment.

Sustainable Materials

From locally sourced stone paving to recycled timber pergolas, material sustainability is gaining traction. Clients are asking more questions about embodied carbon, maintenance requirements, and the provenance of landscape materials. Our team works to source materials that balance aesthetics, durability, and environmental responsibility.
